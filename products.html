Tip #1 Always carry the local currency in cash.

You can always run into different little fees and problems when using a different currency than the local currency. It just makes everything easier to go to an ATM and extract money. Trust me. You'll save more money by doing this.

Tip #2 Hostels are always an option.

Any country you go to, especially in Europe and Asia, you will find hostels. They are a cheaper option and a great way to meet other people who are doing similar things to you. In a room, you have bunkbeds where several different people can stay, so theres not a lot of privacy, but they also almost always have a kitchen and hang out area where you can cook meals or play games in down time. I would not reccommend this option for families. Just get an airbnb if you have kids. It will save headaches and stress.

Tip #3 Don't always do what is easiest.

When I was in New Zealand with my friend, we wanted to go to Cathedral Cove. However, the 'only way' in was by paying close to $100 for a boat tour. We didnt agree with this, so we took the path that was closed due to minor damage (that was easily walked around). We got more of an adventure out of it, could stay as long as we wanted, and paid $0 for it. The easiest option, while it can be fun, is not always the best option, in my opinion. There are more adventures, more of an immersed experience, and its usually cheaper.
